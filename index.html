A short time line of Freddie Mercury's life:

  • 1946: Farrokh Bulsara (Freddie Mercury) was born in Stone Town in the British protectorate of Zanzibar (now part of Tanzania) on 5 September
  • 1963: Mercury and his family fled from Zanzibar for safety reasons due to the Zanzibar Revolution. The family moved into a small house in Feltham, Middlesex, England
  • 1970: Freddie started a long-term relationship with Mary Austin. He lived with Austin for several years in West Kensington, London.
  • 1970: Mercury joined guitarist Brian May and drummer Roger Taylor who had previously been in a band called Smile
  • 1971: Freddie and the others were joined by bassist John Deacon. Despite reservations of the other members Mercury chose the name "Queen" for the new band
  • 1973: Queen signed to a deal with Trident/EMI, and they released their eponymous debut album
  • 1975 Queen recorded and released A Night at the Opera. At the time, it was the most expensive album ever produce. The album also featured the hit single "Bohemian Rhapsody", which was number one in the UK for nine weeks
  • 1976: Freddie told Austin of his sexuality, which ended their romantic relationship
  • 1980: Queen began their 1980s career with The Game. It featured the singles "Crazy Little Thing Called Love" and "Another One Bites the Dust", both of which reached number one in the US
  • 1988: Fans noticed Mercury's increasingly gaunt appearance, than media reported that Mercury was seriously ill with AIDS. Mercury flatly denied this, insisting he was merely "exhausted" and too busy to provide interviews
  • 1991: Innuendo was released in early 1991 with an eponymous number 1 UK hit and other charting singles released later in the year,mwhich included "The Show Must Go On". This song, along with the manner of its lyrics, continued to fuel the media reports that Mercury was dying, although this was still officially denied
  • 1991 On 23 November, in a prepared statement made on his deathbed, Mercury confirmed that he had AIDS. Within 24 hours of the statement, he died of bronchial pneumonia, which was brought on as a complication of AIDS
  • 1997 Queen returned to the studio to record "No-One but You (Only the Good Die Young)", a song dedicated to Mercury and all those who die too soon
